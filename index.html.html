Perspective

Thought leadership

Climate risk is a modelling problem, not just a disclosure one
The gap between TCFD-compliant disclosure and genuine climate risk management is large. Most organisations can describe their physical and transition exposures in narrative terms; very few can quantify them with statistical rigour. The frontier is predictive — regression-based scenario outputs, probabilistic loss estimates, and emissions trajectory models that inform capital allocation, not just annual reports.
TNFD will reshape how financial institutions assess portfolios
Nature-related financial risk is where climate risk was in 2015 — acknowledged but unmeasured. TNFD's LEAP approach and the ENCORE dependency mapping tool are giving financial institutions the infrastructure to assess nature-related exposures for the first time. Early adopters building nature risk into credit assessments and portfolio screening now will be well ahead of the regulatory curve when mandatory TNFD disclosure arrives.
AI agents and automation will redefine ESG data operations
CSRD demands not just reporting but data governance infrastructure — standardised inputs, defined owners, validation protocols, and audit trails. AI agents and Power Automate workflows can dramatically reduce the burden of cross-entity ESG data collection. But automation only works on top of sound data architecture. The organisations that invest in both simultaneously will outpace peers still managing ESG data in spreadsheets.
India's carbon market and the disclosure gap
India's Carbon Credit Trading Scheme represents a structural shift in how Scope 1-heavy industries will account for and disclose emissions. The critical gap today is that most covered entities are not yet TCFD-ready. Early movers who align CCTS compliance with ISSB S2 disclosure architecture will gain a material first-mover advantage with both domestic regulators and international investors.
